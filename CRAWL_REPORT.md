## 4. Visual Style (impressions)
- Minimalist, clean, light backgrounds; lots of whitespace.
- Blurred aesthetic photography + Japanese-themed graphics.
- Low color saturation; professional/modern rather than playful.
- No strong single brand accent observed from the fetch.

## 5. Japanese Text Present
- Minimal. Mainly the brand name “AKEBONO,” explained as meaning **“dawn / new beginning.”** (曙 = akebono.)

## 6. Tone & Audience
- Professional yet welcoming. Targets international learners aiming to study/work in Japan.
- Emphasis on credible, certified instruction; structured learning; community.

## 7. What We Borrowed vs. Changed (anti-copy note)

This project is **inspired, not copied**. No text, images, or unique layouts were reproduced.

**Spirit borrowed (concepts only):**
- The “dawn / new beginning” (曙 / akebono) metaphor → became our visual & emotional theme.
- The trust pillars idea (certified · expert · community) → reinterpreted as our own value cards.
- JLPT N5 batch / level framing → our own N5–N1 class tiers.
- An events/calendar concept → our own weekly schedule timetable.

**Deliberately different:**
- The reference is a commercial academy with paid services, consultations, and webinars.
  **Our site is for FREE classes** — warmer, more personal, single-teacher voice.
- Original copy, original bilingual heading system, original indigo “washi/dawn” design language,
  original components, placeholder imagery (picsum.photos).
